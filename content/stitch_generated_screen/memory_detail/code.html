Midsummer Evening at the Archipelago

June 21, 2023

The air was perfectly still, carrying the faint scent of pine needles and damp earth. We sat on the edge of the weathered wooden dock, feet dangling just above the glassy surface of the water. There were no phones, no notifications—just the rhythmic sound of a distant loon and the slow fading of the amber light.

It felt like time had finally decided to pause, allowing us to simply exist in the quietude of the moment. We spoke in hushed tones, as if raising our voices would shatter the fragile peace of the archipelago. The horizon glowed in shades of bruised purple and soft orange for hours, a testament to the endless days of the northern summer.

I remember the cold bite of the water when I finally decided to dip my toes in, a sharp contrast to the warmth of the coffee mug still held in my hands. These are the moments we archive not for others to see, but for our future selves to remember what it felt like to be truly present.
